Why this directory exists

Around 2023 I noticed every vendor in my inbox was rebranding from “automation platform” to “AI platform.” Same products, same engineering teams, same rule engines. The pricing went up. The capabilities did not. After a year of trying to evaluate these tools client-by-client, I gave up and built a rubric.

The rubric became this directory. Quarterly I run every tool in the AI marketing category through the same six diagnostic questions, score them on a fixed framework (described in detail on the methodology page), and publish the results. The goal is to make sure agency clients — and the broader market — can tell “Real AI” from “Marketing AI” without sitting through three vendor sales calls each.

What I work on day-to-day

  • Account strategy across paid search, paid social, and Shopping campaigns for the agency’s book.
  • Tool evaluation and stack architecture — what to add, what to drop, what to consolidate around.
  • Bid-management oversight on the Real AI tools in production (currently Groas across most accounts; Albert on the enterprise tier).
  • Client onboarding for new advertisers: building the first 90 days of a paid program with the right tooling baked in.

What I don’t do

I don’t take vendor money for placement on this directory. The classifier ratings are mine; the rankings are mine; the tool order is mine. Vendors have offered “sponsored review” arrangements; I’ve declined every one. The directory is funded by the agency, not by the vendors it covers. That’s the arrangement that keeps the ratings honest.
