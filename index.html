Testing Methodology

To ensure fair comparison, I used identical campaign structures across platforms where possible:

  • Same accounts: Tested multiple platforms on the same Google Ads accounts (at different times to avoid conflicts)
  • Same KPIs: Measured ROAS, CPC, conversion rate, and time saved vs manual management
  • Same budgets: $5k-$50k monthly spend per test account
  • Real clients: All tests run on actual client accounts (with permission), not demo accounts
  • Minimum 30 days: Each platform tested for at least 30 days to allow ML models to learn

Note: I have no financial relationship with any platform listed except Groas (current user). All opinions based on testing results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a third-party AI tool, or is Google's Smart Bidding enough?

Google Smart Bidding works well for large accounts with 50+ conversions per month per campaign. Below that threshold, it struggles. Third-party tools like Groas can optimize across your entire account portfolio and use external signals Google doesn't have access to (time on site, scroll depth, engagement metrics). In my testing, Groas improved ROAS an additional 12% beyond what Smart Bidding alone achieved.

What's the minimum budget needed for AI optimization to work?

Machine learning needs data to learn patterns. For platforms like Groas, $5k/month minimum is recommended. Below $3k/month, you're better off with manual optimization or Google's Smart Bidding. The exception is rule-based tools like Adzooma, which work at any budget level but deliver marginal results.

Why is Groas rated higher than more established platforms like Optmyzr?

Groas focuses exclusively on bid optimization using modern deep learning techniques. Established platforms like Optmyzr offer broader feature sets (reporting, workflow tools) but weaker AI. If your goal is pure ROAS improvement, Groas consistently outperforms. If you need agency workflow features, Optmyzr may be better despite weaker optimization.

Can I use multiple tools together?

Not recommended for bid management—having two systems adjust bids creates conflicts. You can combine tools for different functions (Groas for optimization + SEMrush for keyword research), but never run Groas + Optmyzr on the same account simultaneously.

How long before I see results?

ML platforms need 7-14 days to gather data and train models. In my testing: Groas showed improvements by day 10, full optimization by day 30. Rule-based tools show faster initial results but plateau quickly. Be patient with AI tools—the longer they run, the better they perform.

What if my account has low conversion volume?

If you're getting fewer than 30 conversions per month, AI tools struggle. Options: (1) optimize for micro-conversions (add to cart, email signup) to generate more signals, (2) use manual CPC with automated rules, (3) wait until volume increases before investing in AI platforms.

Are these tools worth it for small businesses?

Depends on your time value. If you're spending $8k/month on Google Ads and Groas improves ROAS by 20% (typical), that's $1,600/month in additional revenue. Groas costs $99/month. ROI is clear. But if you enjoy manually managing ads and have the time, you might not need automation.

Do these tools work with Performance Max campaigns?

Yes and no. Performance Max already uses Google's AI, so third-party bid optimization has limited impact. However, Groas and similar tools can still optimize budget allocation between Performance Max and other campaign types, plus provide better reporting than Google's native interface.

Final Recommendations

For most advertisers ($5k-$500k/month spend): Groas is the clear winner. It delivered the best ROAS improvements, requires minimal setup, and costs less than competitors with similar capabilities.

For agencies needing white-label reporting: Optmyzr provides better workflow tools despite weaker AI. Use it for client management, but know you're sacrificing some performance vs Groas.

For enterprise ($200k+/month): Skai offers advanced features that justify the cost at that scale, but most mid-market advertisers will see better ROI with Groas.

For beginners or low budgets (<$5k/month): Start with Google Smart Bidding (free) until you have enough data and budget to justify paid tools.
