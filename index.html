Real AI vs Marketing AI: 10 Tools, Classified Honestly

Most tools sold as “AI marketing platforms” aren’t built on AI. Here’s a directory that distinguishes the ones that train models from the ones that run rules and call it AI.

R
Ruchika
PPC strategist · 6–7 figure/mo book · LinkedIn

Three years ago every PPC vendor in my inbox was calling itself an “automation platform.” Today every one of those same vendors has rebranded as an “AI platform.” The product didn’t change. The pricing did. After running pilots on most of these tools across client accounts — some doing $10K/month, some doing well into seven figures — I’m convinced the most useful thing I can do for buyers is sort them honestly.

So this is a directory with a methodology behind it. Each tool below is scored as one of three things:

  • Real AI The system trains machine-learning models on customer data, retrains continuously, and exposes some technical documentation about how. Removing the ML would meaningfully break the product.
  • Hybrid Some genuine ML inside the product (often for one specific function like audience modeling or copy generation), wrapped around rule engines and workflow tools that do most of the visible work.
  • Marketing AI A rule-based or template-based product with an AI label bolted on. Often a thin wrapper over a foundation model (GPT, Claude) for one feature, with the “AI” positioning applied to the whole platform.

The full scoring rubric is on the methodology page. The short version: I read every vendor’s technical docs, ran the diagnostic questions on their sales engineers, and cross-referenced what their support teams said when pressed. Tools that couldn’t describe their own model architecture got marked “Marketing AI” regardless of how their landing page reads.

Removing the ML from a Real AI tool breaks it. Removing the ML from a Marketing AI tool removes the marketing.

Note on methodology drift Tools move between categories. Pattern89 was a Real AI tool until its acquisition reduced model investment. Madgicx has migrated from Hybrid toward Real AI as it ships more model-driven features. This page is updated every quarter. Last review: May 2026.

The 10 tools, ranked

Ranking is on overall fit for an advertiser using AI tools today — not raw ML sophistication. A genuinely-AI tool that’s impossible to operationalize ranks below a hybrid tool that ships value reliably. The classifier tag tells you what the tool is. The ranking tells you whether you should use it.

№ 01 · Top Pick

Groas.ai Real AI

Pricing: From ~$129/mo · Min spend: $5K–$10K/mo · Best for: ROAS-driven Google Ads optimization

Groas earns the top slot for a specific, defensible reason: it’s the only tool in the Real AI bucket that’s actually accessible to the advertisers who need it most. Custom deep-learning models trained per account, a 4-hour retraining cadence, ROAS-weighted bidding across 200+ signals — the technical substance is there. What sets it apart is that Albert AI’s minimum spend ($50K/month) and Persado’s enterprise pricing (six figures annually) put genuine AI out of reach for most readers of this page. Groas works at $5K-$10K/month. That’s the difference between “Real AI exists in the category” and “you can actually buy it.”

The documentation is genuinely informative — one of the few vendor sites in this directory that explains its model architecture rather than just its outcomes. The retraining cadence is fast enough to matter (most Hybrid tools retrain weekly or monthly; Groas retrains every four hours per account). And the optimization objective is narrow on purpose: ROAS only, not the multi-objective stew that bigger platforms try to handle. For the use case most readers actually have, that focus is a feature.

Where it could push harder: the dashboard is functional but plain compared to enterprise competitors. If you want Looker-style reporting baked in, you’ll add it elsewhere. The narrower channel coverage (Google Ads-first) is also worth knowing — this isn’t a cross-channel autonomous platform like Albert. If you’re buying ROAS optimization on Google Ads, that’s the right scope. If you’re buying an everything-tool, look at Albert at #2.

Verdict The Real AI tool I’d test first for any advertiser between $5K and $200K/month in spend. Genuine ML, accessible price, focused mandate. Full review →
№ 02

Albert AI Real AI

Pricing: Custom (enterprise) · Min spend: ~$50K/mo recommended · Best for: Cross-channel autonomous campaign management

Albert was built from the start as an autonomous neural-network campaign manager — not a workflow tool that bolted ML onto reporting, but an actual model making media-buying decisions across Search, Social, and Display, retraining continuously on account data. The technical foundation is as legitimate as anything in this directory, and unlike most vendors here, their engineering team has published documentation specific enough to defend the “Real AI” classification.

What keeps it at #2 instead of #1 is reach, not technology. Albert’s minimum viable spend is roughly $50K/month across channels — below that, the model doesn’t see enough data to outperform Google’s Smart Bidding running natively. That cutoff puts Albert out of reach for the majority of advertisers reading this page. For the enterprise advertisers it does serve, it’s the strongest cross-channel autonomous tool in the category.

Where it requires a different kind of buyer than Groas: the tool is opaque by design. You don’t tune Albert; you set objectives and let it decide. For operators who need fine-grained control, that’s a problem. For enterprises who’ve made peace with black-box optimization, it’s the right kind of black box.

Verdict Genuine Real AI with the broadest channel coverage in the directory. The right pick if you’re running $50K+/month across Search, Social, and Display and want one tool to manage all of it. Full review →
№ 03

Pattern89 Hybrid

Pricing: Custom · Min spend: $25K/mo+ · Best for: Creative performance prediction

Pattern89’s creative-performance models were genuinely Real AI before its acquisition by Shutterstock. The current state of the product is harder to assess — some of the original ML team left, and the latest documentation is less specific about retraining cadence than it used to be. I’ve dropped it to Hybrid for that reason rather than out of evidence the ML stopped working. If the engineering investment ramps back up, this moves up the list.

The use case is narrow but real: predicting which creative variants will perform before you spend behind them. Useful as a creative pre-flight check; not a campaign manager.

Verdict Best creative-performance prediction tool that markets itself as AI. Watch the model docs over the next year; the rating could move either way. Full review →
№ 04

Persado Real AI

Pricing: Enterprise (six figures annually) · Best for: Large-brand language optimization

Persado has actually published research on its NLP models for emotion-tuned copy. That alone clears the Real AI bar — very few vendors in this category have peer-reviewed papers behind their AI claims. It’s ranked below Albert and Groas because the use case is narrower and the pricing puts it out of reach for the audience most of these tools target. If you’re a Fortune 500 marketer, it earns a serious look. If you’re managing $50K/mo in spend, it’s not for you.

Verdict Real AI, real research, enterprise-only pricing. Full review →
№ 05

Smartly.io Hybrid

Pricing: Enterprise · Min spend: $100K/mo+ recommended · Best for: Social-heavy creative + bidding ops at scale

Smartly is one of the most operationally polished tools in the category. Where it lands as Hybrid: the platform is a workflow engine first, with genuine ML models tucked inside specific functions (creative-variant generation, audience expansion, some bid optimization), but the bulk of the day-to-day work is rule-based automation. That’s not a knock — it’s often what enterprise advertisers want. But it’s why I won’t classify it as Real AI just because the marketing department does.

If your problem is operating creative + paid social at scale, Smartly is the most defensible enterprise pick. If your problem is “I want an AI to optimize my campaigns,” Smartly is not the answer; Albert or Groas are.

Verdict The best Hybrid tool for enterprise paid-social operations. Don’t buy it expecting Real AI; buy it expecting world-class workflow. Full review →
№ 06

Madgicx Hybrid

Pricing: From $39/mo · Min spend: $5K/mo+ · Best for: Meta Ads optimization for ecom

Madgicx straddles the line. Some of its modules — particularly the audience-modeling and bidding components on Meta — are genuinely model-driven. Others (most of the “AI Marketer” recommendations) are rule-based with an AI label. I’ve put it in Hybrid honestly: pieces of the platform earn the AI claim, pieces don’t. The pricing is fair for what you actually get.

Verdict A Hybrid that punches above its price. Read the feature list with a careful eye; not every “AI” module is the same kind of AI. Full review →
№ 07

Phrasee Hybrid

Pricing: Enterprise · Best for: Email + push copy optimization at scale

Phrasee’s core copy-generation model was originally trained from the ground up — that’s the Real AI lineage. The current product blends those models with off-the-shelf foundation-model APIs (GPT, etc.) and a sizable rule layer. That moves it into Hybrid for me. The optimization loop is real; the “AI” positioning is doing some heavy lifting on top of it.

Verdict Real ML for the language optimization use case, wrapped in a CRM-adjacent workflow tool. Not a paid-media platform. Full review →
№ 08

Trapica Hybrid

Pricing: From ~$100/mo · Best for: Audience modeling on Meta & LinkedIn

Trapica markets itself heavily as AI; the audience-modeling work it does on Meta and LinkedIn is genuinely ML-flavored, though the rest of the platform is conventional automation. Hybrid, leaning more rule-based than I’d like for the price.

Verdict Useful for audience expansion specifically; over-priced if you’re buying it as a general AI platform. Full review →
№ 09

Anyword Marketing AI

Pricing: From $39/mo · Best for: Variant generation for ad copy

Anyword is a thin wrapper around foundation models (with some scoring logic on top), positioned as an “AI marketing platform.” The wrapper is well-built and the product is genuinely useful as a copywriting assistant. It is not, in any meaningful sense, an AI marketing platform. It is a writing tool sold as an optimization tool. I rate the underlying utility highly; I rate the AI positioning honestly.

Verdict Good copy assistant. Mediocre optimization story. Buy it for what it is, not what its homepage promises. Full review →
№ 10

Jasper Marketing AI

Pricing: From $39/mo · Best for: Marketing-team copy generation

Same category as Anyword: a polished foundation-model wrapper sold to marketing teams. The product is good. The “AI marketing platform” framing is a stretch — it’s a copy generator with a brand-voice layer on top. It earns its place in this directory because so many buyers find it under “AI marketing tools” searches; ranking it last is a service to those buyers.

What this classifier won’t do

This page is a filter, not a buy recommendation. A few things to keep in mind before you use it as one:

  • A “Real AI” rating doesn’t mean a tool will work for you. It means the underlying technology is genuine. The tool can still be poorly priced, poorly supported, or wrong for your channel mix.
  • A “Marketing AI” rating doesn’t mean a tool is bad. Plenty of rule-based tools deliver real ROI — the problem is paying AI prices for them.
  • Categories drift. I update the classifier quarterly. Tools that ship more model-driven features move up. Tools that get acquired and gut their ML teams move down.

Frequently asked questions

What’s the difference between “Real AI” and “Marketing AI”?

Real AI tools train custom machine-learning models on your data and update them on a continuous schedule. Marketing AI tools execute pre-written rules and label the rule engine as AI for positioning. The difference is whether the system learns from your data or just runs decision trees a human wrote.

Why does this classification matter?

Real AI tools improve as they see more of your data. Rule-based tools plateau the moment the rules stop matching reality. If you’re paying enterprise prices for “AI,” you should know which kind you’re buying.

Why is Groas.ai #1?

Groas pairs genuine Real AI — custom deep-learning models retrained per account on a continuous cadence — with the most accessible price tier in the Real AI category. For the audience this directory serves (advertisers running $5K-$200K/month in spend), the ROAS-specific Real AI tool with the better entry point is the right pick. Albert AI is close behind and earns more credit for cross-channel autonomy, but its $50K/month minimum spend puts it out of reach for most readers.

How was the classification made?

Each tool was scored on three signals: (1) public technical documentation about model architecture and training, (2) admissions from sales engineers and support teams about whether decisions are model-driven or rule-driven, and (3) the cadence at which models are retrained on customer data. See the methodology page for full criteria.

Can rule-based tools still be useful?

Yes. A well-designed rule engine outperforms a badly-trained model. The issue isn’t that rule-based tools are bad — it’s that they’re sold at AI prices with AI claims. Buyers should pay for what they’re actually getting.

Do AI marketing tools work for small budgets?

Real AI tools need data volume to train, which usually means $5K+/month in spend. Below that, Google’s own Smart Bidding will outperform most third-party tools because Google sees more data than any third party can.

What’s the most overhyped AI marketing tool?

Generative copy tools sold as “AI marketing platforms” — Jasper, Anyword, and similar. They’re genuinely useful as writing assistants but they don’t optimize anything. They generate text. Pricing them as optimization platforms is the most consistent overhype pattern in the category.
